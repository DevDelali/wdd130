Cape Cost

The castle was originally built by Swedes in 1653 and was later taken over by the Dutch and then the British. It is one of several forts and castles along the coast of Ghana that were built by European traders. Cape Coast Castle was a significant site during the transatlantic slave trade, serving as a holding area for enslaved people before they were shipped to the Americas.
